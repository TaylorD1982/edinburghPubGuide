Edinburgh's 30 Best Pubs - 2024

  • Jolly Judge

    The Jolly Judge sits at the foot of an Edinburgh ‘close’, at the centre of one of the capital’s historic Old Town, just 2 minutes’ walk from the Castle.

    An independently-owned bar with low beamed ceilings, a warm fire and a tranquil atmosphere – this is Scottish hospitality at its best. Sample the wide range of malt whiskies, real ales and tempting bar lunches.

    Address: 7 James Court, Lawnmarket, Edinburgh, United Kingdom

    Opening Hours: 12.00 - 23.00

  • Cumberland

    The Cumberland Bar has been a long established favourite public house and restaurant in Edinburgh's New Town. In previous times, the bar was known as "The Tilted Wig", owned and operated by the well known Paddy Crossan. During his ownership the bar became a frequent lunchtime and evening stop for the areas lawyers, judges and other professional inhabitants.

    In the mid 1990's, the equally well known Ian Whyte purchased the bar and renovated it with the style and quality of finish that he was already famous for. Detailed woodwork with an emphasis on real ale and development of the food operation established it as the New Town's finest local and won CAMRA's best pub refurbishment award.

    Address: 1-3 Cumberland Street, Edinburgh, EH3 6RT

    Opening Hours: Monday to Thursday, 12 noon - 11pm / Friday to Sunday, 12 Noon - 12pm

  • Bennet's

    Bennets Bar in Tollcross (not to be confused with Bennets of Morningside also in Edinburgh and run by Bennets Bar’s previous landlord) remains one of the finest pubs in Edinburgh.

    Bennets Bar on Leven Street is an historic, ornate pub, now under the direct control of ‘Scottish and Newcastle’. A drinking establishment of some description has stood here since 1839, however it has not always been Bennets Bar. The current pub was opened in 1906 when the King’s Theatre replaced the Taylor MacLeod brewery next door. Since then, not much has changed.

    Address: 7 James Court, Lawnmarket, Edinburgh, United Kingdom

    Opening Hours: 12.00 - 23.00

  • Bannermans

    Hosting bands and solo artists seven nights a week, we are dedicated to bringing you the very best in up-and-coming local talent, rising national stars and famous acts from around the world.

    Every genre of music is welcome, and we pride ourselves on providing everything a band needs for a performance with a full backline, 5K P.A. and lighting rig, and some of the best sound engineers in town.

    Our friendly and knowledgeable staff are always happy to help out with any of your requirements or needs, so if you're simply grabbing a quiet pint in the afternoon, having a big night out, playing or putting on your first gig, or even just curious, come and check us out – you won’t be disappointed!

    Address: 212 Cowgate, Edinburgh, EH1 1NQ

    Opening Hours: 12pm - 1am

  • Thistle Street Bar

    The Thistle Street Bar has gone through many incarnations since the property was first built around 1775. As a pub it was known as Marr’s Bar in the late 1970s and early 80s (the late Archie Marr being one of the early band members of the Bay City Rollers). Going back even further it was known as Just Charlie’s.

    But, who were the people who lived at the property in the first street built in Edinburgh’s New Town during the Georgian and Victorian periods? Edinburgh historian Barclay Price has done some digging on our behalf and here is the result of his findings. We hope you will enjoy exploring the history of 39 Thistle Street.

    Address: 7 James Court, Lawnmarket, Edinburgh, United Kingdom

    Opening Hours: 12.00 - 23.00

  • Kay's Bar

    John Kay and Sons, Wine Merchants traded from 39 Jamaica Street supplying fine wines and spirits for the domestic and international market. Their closure in the seventies sadly concluded over 150 years of excellence, which had earned the company immense respect. Thereafter trading as a Public House, the mantle of John Kay has been upheld. Unerring attention to quality has culminated in acknowledgement as Edinburgh’s Premier Cask Ale-House.

    Kay’s is an exceptionally well-preserved Victorian Bar, with many interesting features throughout the premises. The internal signage around the frieze, the barrels, panelling, cast-iron pillars and the safe are some of the original fittings.

    Address: 7 James Court, Lawnmarket, Edinburgh, United Kingdom

    Opening Hours: 12.00 - 23.00

  • Barony

    A must-see restoration of an Edinburgh institution, the art-deco inspired Barony first opened as a drinks merchant in 1896 before becoming a pub and it has been a staple of the Edinburgh food & drink scene ever since.

    From nips and drams, to real ale and a great glass of wine, The Barony is a well-loved and well-known watering hole and gastro-pub for locals and visitors alike.

    Address: 7 James Court, Lawnmarket, Edinburgh, United Kingdom

    Opening Hours: 12.00 - 23.00
